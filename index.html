About the Project
Transforming Plantation Forestry for a Sustainable Future

Rubberwood processing operations in Southeast Asia

Laos is a Least Developed Country with nearly 25% of its population below the poverty line. While 70% of the Lao population works in agriculture, the Government of Laos has identified plantation forestry and wood processing as key opportunities to alleviate poverty, increase tree cover, and meet climate change objectives.

Rubber plantations represent approximately 58% of Lao plantation area, covering around 300,000 hectares. The majority are expected to become senescent by 2030. Currently, this timber is often wasted or burned as low grade fuel. VALTIP4 works to convert this valuable resource into high value engineered wood products for domestic and export markets.

Global demand for wood products is projected to quadruple over the next 30 years. Laos is well positioned to expand its forestry and forest products manufacturing sectors, capturing value adding market opportunities that neighboring countries like Vietnam and Thailand have already seized.

Project Objectives
Six Strategic Objectives Driving Change

Our multi disciplinary approach addresses policy, technology, markets, gender equity, and workplace safety to create lasting impact across the forestry value chain.

01

Policy & Governance

Strengthen the enabling environment for plantation expansion. Support smallholders and MSMEs through improved policy, regulation, and industry frameworks.

02

WTRC Self Sufficiency

Build the Wood Technical Research Centre at NUoL into a fully self supporting, certified, fee for service testing and training facility.

03

Timber R&D

Address technical barriers in wood product recoveries, optimise rubberwood processing, and develop bio based adhesives and circular economy practices.

04

Markets & Supply Chains

Build market intelligence and forecasting of global and regional demand trends to identify export and domestic opportunities matched to Lao resources.

05

Gender & Social Inclusion

Examine the effects of industry development on female livelihoods, particularly for ethnic minority women, and improve equity across the supply chain.

06

Occupational Health & Safety

Establish routine industrial hygiene and OH&S protocols across forest and wood processing industries, addressing significant safety gaps.

Research Streams
Six Integrated Research Streams

A comprehensive multi disciplinary research program spanning policy, technology, markets, social inclusion, and workplace safety.

Stream 01

Policy, Governance & Industry

Strengthening policy frameworks and institutional capacity for sustainable forest industry expansion in Laos. Supporting smallholders and MSMEs through improved regulation.

Led by Dept. of Forestry & ANU
Stream 02

WTRC at NUoL

Building the Wood Technical Research Centre into a fully self supporting, certified testing and training facility for Lao and regional forest industries.

National University of Laos
Stream 03

Timber R&D & Circular Economy

Addressing technical barriers in wood product recoveries with focus on senescent rubberwood, bio based adhesives, and circular economy practices.

Griffith Uni & Melbourne
Stream 04

Supply Chains & Markets

Building market intelligence and forecasting of global and regional trends. Identifying export and domestic opportunities for Lao plantation resources.

Salwood Asia Pacific
Stream 05

Gender & Social Inclusion

Examining gender effects of industry development to improve equity across the supply chain, with focus on ethnic minority women in Laos.

NUoL Social Sciences
Stream 06

Occupational Health & Safety

Establishing routine OH&S protocols in forest and wood processing industries. Exchange visits with Australian regulators and on site training programs.

DPI Queensland
Project Phases
Five Year Roadmap

From inception and baseline studies through to completion and handover, each phase builds systematically toward lasting impact and institutional sustainability.

YEAR 1 — July 2025 to June 2026

Inception & Baseline

Establish project steering committee, recruit in country manager, conduct inception workshop, and develop full M&E framework with baseline data collection.

OH&S Review Market Intelligence WTRC SWOT Policy Forums
YEAR 2 — July 2026 to June 2027

Research Build Up

Scale up in country research, begin plantation resource characterisation studies focusing on rubberwood. Deliver first WTRC business model and financial plan.

Resource Reports WTRC Business Model Export Logistics
YEAR 3 — July 2027 to June 2028

Mid Term & Expansion

Mid term review to assess progress. Begin EWP product testing and wood product technical trials. Intensify OH&S on site training. Gender mid term review.

EWP Trials Bio Adhesives MSME Support
YEAR 4 — July 2028 to June 2029

Consolidation

Consolidate research findings across all streams. Deliver second round resource characterisation and circular economy trial reports. Support WTRC self sustaining operations.

Final Reports Circular Economy Labour Study
YEAR 5 — July 2029 to August 2030

Completion & Handover

Finalise all technical research. Complete and commission the EWP demonstration building at NUoL. Confirm WTRC self sufficiency pathway. Final M&E evaluation.

EWP Building MSME Manuals Project Close

Expected Impact
Transformative Outcomes

From economic growth and environmental sustainability to gender equity and institutional capacity building.

Economic Growth

Expanded wood processing volumes, increased profitability, more employment, and greater national revenue for Laos.

Environmental

Expanded plantation area, increased carbon sequestration, and substitution of wood products for energy intensive materials.

Social Equity

Improved gender equity, better OH&S practices, and greater inclusion of ethnic minority women in research and employment.

Institutional

A self sustaining WTRC at NUoL providing testing, certification and training services to Lao and regional forest industries long term.
